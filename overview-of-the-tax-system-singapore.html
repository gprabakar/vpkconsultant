Overview of the Tax System in Singapore

The tax system is a structured framework through which governments collect revenue from individuals and businesses to fund public services and infrastructure. Understanding the tax system is critical for both taxpayers and policymakers as it affects economic behavior and financial planning.

Types of Taxes

There are several types of taxes that governments impose, each with its unique characteristics and purposes:

Income Taxes

Income taxes are levied on the income of individuals and corporations. They are typically progressive, meaning that the tax rate increases as the taxable amount increases. Key components of income taxes include:

  • Personal Income Tax: Paid by individuals on wages, salaries, and other income.
  • Corporate Income Tax: Charged on a corporation's profits.
